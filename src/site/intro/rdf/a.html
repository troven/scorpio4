Everything in Scorpio4 is described by a URI. So, let's take a look at them in more detail. There are three distinct types of URI, each with their own use cases:

URL (Uniform Resource Locator)

URLs are, by definition, internet protocols. They locate and retrieve data within the Internet's address space. The "cloud" extends to your intranet, and your home wifi too.

We all know that the http: is immensely powerful. It's the world wide web. But that's only a tiny part of the Internet address space.

There are many others, such as smb:, imap:, ssh: and ftp:. There are hundreds of protocols, each potentially as powerful. What would you do if you could completely control everything on your personal Internet?

There are protocols for databases, file servers, instant messaging, network monitoring and telephony. The Scorpio4 project is committed to making them all accessible.

Scorpio4 supports physical protocols, like http: & file: as well as pseudo-protocols like sql: or even salesforce:. In most cases, you need not be concerned about the mechanism employed to resolve the protocols.

Just as with http:, the other protocols often employ ?named=values to represent stateful parameters.

ExampleProtocolParametersComments
http://scorpio4demo.com/hello/world/ HTTP: n/a Points to a resource (/hello/world/) collection on a server hosted at scorpio4demo.com
smb://myserver.local/hello/world/ SMB: n/a Points to a folder (/hello/world/) on on a file server called myserver.local
snmp://myprinter.local:1.3.6.1.2.1.25.3.5.1.2 SNMP: n/a Retrieves the hrPrinterDetectedErrorState from myprinter.local for any network printer.
imap://joe@example.com/INBOX/;uid=20/;internal:91354a473744909de610943775f92038 IMAP: n/a Refers to one of Joe's email messages stored in his example.com INBOX.

URN (Uniform Resource Notation)

A URN is useful but not as capable as the more familiar URL. It is a local identifier that points to a resource within a self contained system. You may know them variously as GUIDs or UUIDs.

They all begin with the urn: prefix. Scorpio4 reserves these for your internal concepts and entities. They should be employed with reservation.

It's common practice to use MD5 or SHA1 keys, for example: urn:uuid:43A368A6-4FEEF833-9CCF389A-5CE9F1B4-C5D5637A

Blank Nodes (Anonymous Resources)

Blank Nodes are used inside collections, such as a lists and sets to identify the members. In our examples, they are automatically generated by the N3 notation.

While often mis-understood, and a little tricky to grasp, they prove useful in practice.

Blank Nodes are often hidden, but they look like :_b123123. The leading : identifies they are local within some external context. It unsafe to refer to them outside of their specific context.
