Building Knowledge

RDF is very flexible, each fact within a statement may refer to other facts. When these triples are logically combined, they create a web-of-data. 

        @PREFIX     eg:        <http://scorpio4demo.com/example/#>.

        :sally      eg:knows    :harry.

A giant global graph as a data structure is a powerful idea. It can express almost any data, and any relationship.

We can, and should, use triples to express both data and meta-data about our problem domain.

Self Describing

With a URI, you can recursively keep asking for more information until you have what you need, or exhaust your search.

In this example, we could start asking facts about :harry or adding facts about :sally. We can ask about eg:knows. We have a well defined relationship between our facts.

Literal Facts

So, URIs are useful, we also need to express simpler literal facts. 


        @PREFIX     eg:        <http://scorpio4demo.com/example/#>.
        @PREFIX     rdf:       <http://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#>.
        @PREFIX     :          <http://scorpio4demo.com/example/eg01#>.

        # Our first paragraph in N3 notation:

        :sally      eg:name     "Sally Smith".
        :sally      eg:DoB      "1972-07-13"^^xsd:date.

Prefix Expansion

Remember, each of the non-literal terms is defined by a URI. Internally, the prefixes are joined with the suffix to create a fully qualified URI. 

      http://scorpio4demo.com/example/eg01#harry
      http://scorpio4demo.com/example/eg01#sally
      http://scorpio4demo.com/example/#name
      http://scorpio4demo.com/example/#DoB

The N3 notation offers a number of conveniences apart from PREFIX. Often we want to state many facts about a common subject.

In this case, N3 allows us to declare the subject once, by using a ; semi-colon.

        :sally      rdf:type    eg:Person;
                    eg:name     "Sally Smith";
                    eg:DoB      "1972-07-13"^^xsd:date.

Notice, that we finished our paragraph with a . period. The next statement must now specify the subject afresh.
