Source Of Truth

A Source Of Truth (SoT) refers to the practice of structuring data and meta-data so that every element is known by a single authoritative identity, stored in a single definitive location.

Scorpio4 implements a number of tools and strategies to assist with the creation, maintenance and exploitation of an executable source of truth (ESoT)

Any links or uses of an information element (possibly in a SQL schema, federated or exotic data stores) are by reference only.

Thus, when any such ESoT element is modified, the update propagates to the dependent systems throughout enterprise.

Creating an ESoT

  • Import definitions into a semantic store.
  • Collect the desired data.
  • link data elements together and to their definitions.
  • Measure and analyze the data against known standards and benchmarks.
  • Derive inter-dependent use cases such as physical schemas, business rules and user interfaces.

Master Data Management

Scorpio4 supports multiple models per business entity. A person is not one-dimensional. We are parents, friends, colleagues. With each party, we have different motivations and requirements.

Each element within the ESoT can be linked to any other, furthermore rich meta-data about the relationship can be captured too.

Scorpio4 employs an RDF/SPARQL repository to store data and meta-data about the enterprise, it's infrastructure and the integration between data, people and processes. Your data is not locked into a proprietary format, nor does Scorpio4 mandate a particular programming style or choice of language.

We think in terms of aligning information entities to a model of engagement defined by the business. We encourage flexibility, different situations call for different approaches. Scorpio4 captures and then lets you exploit the relationships between the data to rapidly deploy robust, scalable and reliable systems.

Whether you are building a rich end user experience or system-to-system integration, Scorpio4 is tooled up and ready to roll.

Systems Integration

A common approach to working with disparate systems is to employ a Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA) that employs an Enterprise Service Bus (ESB).

A SOA/ESB allows services to interact and inter-operate without the traditional complexity, or strong coupling.

Scorpio4 supports industry standard integration patterns (EIP) with technologies including REST, SOAP, MQ, XMPP and dozens more.

Knowledge Warehouse

A Knowledge Warehouse stores data and meta-data that has, or can be, reshaped and re-purposed to meet different business requirements.

A Knowledge Warehouse is fed from multiple systems, so it is not an ESoT but rather each data element refers to the ESoT.

The Knowledge Warehouse stores meta-data about your data environment. It can replicate data too making the repository a richly, query-friendly, self-describing storage engine.

When we link knowledge with actions, we call that Wisdom Management. Scorpio4 finally enables us to talk in terms of an Enterprise Wisdom Warehouse.
